#
# Burnham and Anderson (2002) write that delta difference (dAIC) for models
# within 0-2 of a min have a substantial support; deltas within 4-7 considerably less
# support and deltas greater than 10 essentially have no support. These
# support-levels are intended to be used for selecting a range of models
# around a minimum model for model averaging. And Burnham and Anderson might cringe
# a little at the inverse interpretation below, where we test for the alternative:
# whether a model is significantly different from a null (no habitat covs) model.
# But this follows the model-based hypothesis testing of Royle and Dorazio
# (Ch. 2.5), except that we are not using a frequentist test statistic
# (e.g., Wald or LRT).
#
